Quartz Watches

Product Decsription

The most common movement in modern watches, a quartz watch is powered by an electronic movement with a tiny quartz crystal integrated into the electronics of the watch. Due to the properties of quartz, the crystal will oscillate at a practically perfect frequency when an electronic current is run through it. This makes quartz watches incredibly accurate. Higher end quartz watches (Seiko for example) can stay accurate to within a few seconds a year. Shop our wide selection of quartz watches today!

A quartz movement utilizes a battery as its primary power source and is typically the type of movement that you will find in your standard, no-frills watch. To create power in quartz watch movements, a battery sends an electrical current through a small quartz crystal, electrifying the crystal to create vibrations. These vibrations keep the movement oscillating and drive the motor to move the watch hands.
